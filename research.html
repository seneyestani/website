Here you can find some research projects I have worked on:

Brown Carbon light absorption in a chemical transport model

Wildfires are the major emission source of carbonaceous aerosol in the United States. In this study, we employed a comprehensive set of remote sensing observations to constrain the representation of wildfire carbonaceous aerosol in a chemical transport model. Our analysis demonstrates the importance of accounting for light absorption by brown carbon and secondary organic aerosol formation in order to reconcile model output with observations.
More details: doi.org/10.1039/D1EA00059D

Regional climate and health effects of GDI engines

We used a regional climate model over the U.S. to evaluate radiative and health effects of shifting from PFI to GDI engine technology in gasoline cars. The results show that switching all gasoline cars' fleet to GDI engine cause a noticeable regional warming. Also, PM2.5-related annual deaths due to gasoline vehicles would be almost double after shifting to GDI over the U.S.
More details: doi.org/10.1021/acs.est.9b04115

Dust storms modeling in the Middle East

In this project, we used a combination of satellite remote sensing data and in-situ air pollution measurements to detect dust storms over the Middle East. Then a regional climate model is employed to simulte dust storms emission and distribution. The results show a strong effect of dust storms on air quality and radiation. Furthermore, dust emission in Iran has a small contribution to major emission sources in the region.
More details: doi.org/10.1007/s11869-017-0494-8
